The story of Marselisborg Memorial Park


Marselisborg Memorial Park was inaugurated in 1925 as an approx. 15 hectare area sloping
towards the Bay of Aarhus and with a view of Skødshoved and Mols. To the south and
north, the area was bounded by old beech forest and other vegetation.

Lots of different trees and shrubs were planted in the park. It was originally the idea that
the park should be a gathering place for Danes abroad when they were visiting their home
country. However, this plan did not really get a foothold, partly because the Rebild parties
at Rold Skov were so popular.

Memorial monument for the First World War

If you enter the memorial, you enter a large circular space. On the walls of the monument are engraved
the names of the 4,144 people from Southern Jutland who, as Danish-minded people, had to fight in
German uniforms and subsequently fell in the First World War.

Close to Marselisborg Castle

As an extension to Marselisborg Memorial Park, you will find Marselisborg Castle, which contains a long
and exciting history. The park is one of many beautiful parks in Aarhus .

Trot, Monté & Canter at Jutland Racecourse

You can experience up to 35 race days a year, where you can both follow the horses or
participate in one of the many audience competitions.
You can keep up to date with the program on Jydsk Væddeløpsbane's website .

For the children

Children are of course also welcome, and while the parents follow the horse races, there
are both indoor and outdoor playgrounds, pony rides (on race days), and a home cinema.

Establishment of Forest Botanic Garden

It was an unattractive piece of forest, but even so forester Niels Johan Bang had taken a liking
to the area. The many different soil types made the area ideal for an aboret – a collection of different
trees and bushes. In addition, part of the land was on service land belonging to the forestry farm
Louisenlund , which meant that it was literally in Bang's backyard.

Forester Bang managed to get the city council on board with the idea, and in 1923 the Forest
Botanic Garden was established. At the same time, DKK 2,000 was set aside annually in the budget
for forest botanical experiments, so that the tree collection could be continuously expanded.
Although DKK 2,000 in the mid-1920s corresponded to just under DKK 60,000, it was still a modest
budget that woodcutter Bang had to start his collection. Together with his forester, Knudsgaard ,
he rolled up his sleeves, and after just six years the garden had over 1,000 different species and
varieties in its front garden. Many of the trees and bushes were obtained through Bang's circle of
acquaintances, who had taken an interest in the project.
